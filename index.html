What we're learning now:

What I've learned

In this program I've learned to accept that I can't be the best at eveything. Okay, let me correct myself i'm trying to accept that i'm not going to be the best at everything. It reminded of my transition from from 8th grade to High School. I was an overachiever who had never seen anything lower than an A- before the beg/mid of 10th grade. I got a B+ and it completely crushed me. I kid you not I actually cried. I thought I was stupid and didn't even want to try anymore. That was wrong I was in a fixed mindset then. I thought I was already in a growth mindest, but hadn't really gotten there until I had to struggle to be "good" at something. Now I truly understand that anyone can be good at anything as long as they're are willing to work and practice hard enough.

This program has also helped with time management and responsibility. This is the first time I have had to actually use public tranportation to get from place to place. Which has been a very frustrating experience i'm glad I was forced to go through. Despite my struggles I've still learned a lot in short my time here. Should I work in CS I would definitely be a front-end developer. Though, I don't think i'll be looking for a career in CS. Career goal or not CS skills are useful in my everyday life and I'm glad that I have them.
